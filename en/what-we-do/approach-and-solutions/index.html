Waste to Energy treatment for landfills

Government owned entities are usually responsible for the Waste Management on the Caribbean Islands. Waste disposal currently takes place on Open Dumpsites, which are a health and environmental hazard. They typically have little to no formal management or measures in place to protect the environment, leading to high hidden costs associated with air pollution, water pollution, health risks, flooding and more.

It is of utmost importance to keep improving the Landfill Waste Management activities in accordance with available Waste Directive, Landfill Directive and the Waste Hierarchy. This to prevent, or reduce the potential impact of, pollution of the environment or harm to human health and encourage sustainable use of resources.

To accomplish the above objectives, the following steps must be taken:

  • Transition from open dumpsites to sanitary landfill
  • Installing landfill gas extraction wells
  • Using waste processing/valorization technologies to reduce waste volume and creating value added products.

The BluEconomy organisation together with its strategic partners can support the Government owned entities with several activities like local waste analysis including landfill, and set up of a (turn-key) development plan. The required investments will be a subject of discussion with several opportunities as well..
